SDR Rules of Engagement & Performance Governance

Governance framework to reduce ownership ambiguity, prevent duplicate outreach, and standardize accountability.

Context

  • Sole SDR Team Lead managing 11–15 SDRs supporting 7–15 Account Executives across multiple products and regions.
  • No defined expiration logic for lead ownership.
  • No structured cross-product enforcement model.
  • Performance management lacked formal intervention standards.

Structural Problems

  • Duplicate outreach risk due to ownership ambiguity.
  • Cross-sell conflicts across active opportunities.
  • Escalations caused by unclear communication boundaries.
  • Inconsistent performance accountability.

Architecture Built

  • Lead Ownership Lifecycle: SDR self-assignment allowed for exactly 14 days at the Lead object level.
  • Salesforce Flow Enforcement: Automated removal of ownership at day 14; relabeled as expired; no manual override permitted.
  • Opportunity Guardrails: Decision-tree framework evaluating open opportunity stage, product overlap, and protected contacts.
  • Cross-Sell Controls: Allowed expansion outreach while protecting contacts tied to active opportunities.
  • PIP Framework: Formalized performance improvement standards triggered by defined quota thresholds; documented in Confluence and integrated into onboarding.

Governance & Enforcement

  • Automation handled ownership expiration.
  • Structured onboarding reinforced decision logic.
  • Direct intervention enforced violations immediately.
  • No reliance on informal memory-based rules.

Impact

  • Eliminated duplicate demo booking incidents tied to ownership ambiguity.
  • Reduced internal escalations across SDR and AE teams.
  • Standardized performance accountability across up to 15 SDRs.
  • Created repeatable governance framework for onboarding.

Next Improvements

  • Automated stale-opportunity detection.
  • Violation logging dashboard.
  • Additional object-level protections without restricting productivity.
