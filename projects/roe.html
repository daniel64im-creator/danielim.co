SDR Rules of Engagement & Performance Governance

Governance framework to reduce ownership ambiguity, prevent duplicate outreach, and standardize accountability.

Context

  • Sales Development team environment with multiple SDRs and multiple AEs across products and regions.
  • No consistent rules for lead ownership expiration, cross-product boundaries, or performance intervention.

Structural Problems

  • Ownership ambiguity caused internal conflict and duplicate outreach risk.
  • Cross-sell rules were inconsistent and hard to enforce.
  • Performance management lacked consistent standards and documentation.

What I Built

  • Lead ownership lifecycle rule: SDRs could assign themselves as Lead Owner for a fixed 14-day window.
  • Salesforce Flow enforcement: Ownership was automatically removed at day 14 and labeled as expired (no manual override).
  • Opportunity and cross-sell guardrails: Documented decision logic for when outreach is allowed and which contacts must be protected.
  • PIP framework: Documented performance improvement standards and timelines in collaboration with leadership/HR (formalized and stored as internal documentation).

Impact

  • Reduced duplicate outreach incidents and escalations caused by unclear boundaries.
  • Created repeatable governance standards for onboarding and enforcement.
  • Made accountability expectations explicit instead of informal.

What I Would Improve Next

  • Automated stale-opportunity checks to reduce manual review.
  • Lightweight logging/dashboarding for violations and exceptions.
  • Stronger object-level protections where appropriate (without over-restricting reps).
