SDR Rules of Engagement & Governance

Enforceable ownership boundaries, cross-sell controls, and performance governance for a multi-product SDR org.

Environment

Multi-product GTM org with shared accounts across product teams.

Team Served

11–15 SDRs; 7–15 AEs; San Jose + Toronto.

Primary Systems

Salesforce (Lead/Contact/Opportunity), Confluence, Canvas training.

Core Mechanism

14-day lead ownership lifecycle with automated expiration.

Problem

  • No consistent ownership boundaries → duplicate outreach and internal conflict.
  • Unclear cross-product rules → SDRs unsure who could contact whom and when.
  • Performance management lacked structure → inconsistent coaching, unclear triggers, and escalation ambiguity.
  • Governance existed as “tribal knowledge” rather than enforceable rules.

Design Goals

  • Make ownership enforceable (not optional).
  • Reduce judgment calls with decision trees.
  • Allow cross-sell where safe while protecting active deal contacts.
  • Create a repeatable performance framework tied to measurable thresholds.

System Architecture

1) Lead Ownership Lifecycle (14-day rule)

  • SDR can assign themselves as Lead Owner for exactly 14 days.
  • Ownership cannot be extended by “touches” or manual re-assignments.
  • At day 14, automation removes ownership and relabels the lead as expired/eligible.

2) Opportunity Guardrails (decision-tree governance)

  • Defined what “active opportunity protection” meant and how to evaluate it.
  • Protected contacts actively involved in open opportunities from cross-product outreach.
  • Allowed outreach into the same account for other products when protected contacts were avoided.
  • Included recency logic (e.g., activity windows) to reduce subjective interpretation.

3) Cross-Sell Controls

  • Clear boundaries on who is off-limits vs eligible within an account.
  • Standardized handling for parallel motions across products.
  • Documented “do not contact” conditions and reinforced during onboarding.

4) Performance Governance (PIP framework)

  • Formalized triggers tied to quarterly attainment (e.g., <80%).
  • Defined timeline, expectations, and measurable KPIs during improvement windows.
  • Aligned manager actions with HR expectations to reduce inconsistency.

Enforcement Layer

Principle: Governance fails when it depends on memory, good intent, or “common sense.”
  • Automation: Salesforce Flow enforced ownership expiration and prevented informal overrides.
  • Documentation: Rules published in Confluence; converted into visual workflows.
  • Training: Packaged into a Canvas training module for onboarding consistency.
  • Manager review: Violations addressed immediately in 1:1 to reinforce boundaries.

Outcomes

  • Reduced duplicate outreach and internal escalation events tied to ownership disputes.
  • Converted “unwritten rules” into a repeatable operating system for SDR execution.
  • Increased consistency in onboarding and governance comprehension across locations.
  • Created a defensible performance management structure aligned with leadership/HR.

What I Would Improve Next

  • Automated stale-opportunity detection to clarify when protections should expire.
  • Violation tracking dashboard (trendline by rule type, team, time period).
  • Additional validation rules to prevent common “workaround” behaviors.
  • Tighter instrumentation: log rule checks and outcomes rather than relying on manual review.
