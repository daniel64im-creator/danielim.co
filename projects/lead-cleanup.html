Conference Lead Cleanup & Redistribution

Structured intake, normalization, and routing process to convert raw event lists into actionable outreach queues.

Context

  • Event leads were delivered as raw CSV lists with inconsistent formatting.
  • No standardized routing logic tied to product or team structure.
  • Without cleanup, follow-up speed and ownership clarity suffered.

Structural Problems

  • Duplicate records across events and existing CRM leads.
  • Inconsistent field formatting (name casing, company formatting, product notes).
  • No clear ownership assignment before distribution.
  • Risk of delayed outreach after time-sensitive events.

Architecture Built

  • Data Normalization: Cleaned and standardized names, company fields, and core contact data.
  • Deduplication Pass: Removed obvious duplicates prior to redistribution.
  • Product Categorization: Segmented leads by stated product interest or contextual relevance.
  • Ownership Routing: Assigned leads into appropriate team/queue buckets before handoff.
  • Structured Redistribution: Ensured SDRs received clean, clearly owned outreach lists immediately.

Governance & Controls

  • Manual review layer before redistribution to reduce routing errors.
  • Clear ownership labeling to prevent duplicate follow-up.
  • Defined intake flow instead of ad hoc list sharing.

Impact

  • Accelerated post-event follow-up speed.
  • Reduced duplicate outreach from improperly assigned records.
  • Improved accountability by clarifying who owned which leads.
  • Created repeatable intake process for future events.

Next Improvements

  • Standardized pre-event intake template required from marketing.
  • Automated duplicate detection prior to assignment.
  • Formal routing documentation with decision-tree logic.
